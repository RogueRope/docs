### Aftercare & Integration

Rogue Rope Camp can be intense. The soft landing matters.

![Blankets, water, and soft support for the landing after intensity.](/images/rrc26/mj/7._Aftercare_illustration_blankets_water_cushions_soft_touch__6d7b0592-04f8-4d58-9b37-5d11b41457de_1.png)

---

## Before You Leave Camp

- Eat something real. Drink water. Sleep if you can.
- If you're wobbly, ask early. Find an [FWB](/docs/wellbeing/fwb/), a friend, or the team.
- If you made a tender connection, say a clean goodbye (or agree how you'll check in later).

## The First 48 Hours Back

- Expect a shift. Euphoria, fog, grief, irritation, hunger, loneliness can all show up.
- Keep your calendar light if you can. Don't stack big decisions right away.
- Move your body a little. Eat. Shower. Go outside. Basic care counts.

## The First Week

- Write down what you want to remember (not the whole story, just the truth of it).
- If you want to message someone from camp, do it simply and without pressure.
- If something felt off, talk it through with someone grounded. Don't spiral alone.

## If It Hits Hard

If you feel unsafe, overwhelmed, or destabilized, reach out to someone you trust and consider professional support in your country.

If something happened at camp that needs follow-up, use [Contact](/docs/contact/). We would rather hear about it than have you carry it alone.
