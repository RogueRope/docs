## Dress Code: Dust, Roots & Wild Things

We're sleeping under the sky.  
The land is holding us.  
The play party theme follows that logic.

**Dust, Roots & Wild Things** — our invitation for the main evening.

Think: earth tones and exposed skin. Fur and leather. Things tied with cord and wire. Things that look like they were found, not bought. Bodies that remember they came from somewhere older than the internet.

This is not a costume competition.  
This is not a runway.  
This is a portal — and the dress code is your ticket in.

---

## Archetypes to Inspire

**Dust & Earth:**  
Linen, leather, suede. Browns, terracottas, deep greens. Something worn-in. Something that could have been in the ground once.

**Roots & Rope:**  
Woven, tied, wrapped. Fiber as decoration. Rope as garment. Make it look like you got dressed in a forest and didn't mind.

**Wild Things:**  
Animal energy. Horns. Claws. Markings. Fur. Feathers. The part of you that doesn't apologize for taking up space.

**Ritual & Sacred:**  
White linen. Flowers. Symbols. The version of you that exists in ceremony, not just ordinary life.

**Shapeshifters:**  
Mix everything. Layer fur over lace. Combine elements that shouldn't work and do. Let the look evolve over the evening.

---

## The Only Real Rules

**Dress for ease, for touch, for breath.**  
You'll be tying and being tied. Warmth may matter — August nights can surprise you.  
Wear what makes you feel like yourself. Or the self that only comes out when the fire's burning and the space feels safe.

This is an **invitation, not a requirement.**  
Dark comfortable clothes + presence = you belong.

---

## Consent Is Sacred Magic

How much or how little you dress up is entirely your call.  
The portal opens wide — whether you arrive covered in earth paint, dressed in denim, or wrapped in nothing but rope.

We don't enforce the theme. We invite it.  
The people who play with it tend to find something.  
But you're already welcome, exactly as you are.
