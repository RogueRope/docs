## A Field. A Fire. Home for Six Days.

The campsite is simple — just how we like it.  
A field with trees, a firepit, some rope hanging between branches, and space to build something weird and wonderful together.

No glamping. No assigned spots. Just shared ground and a little chaos magic.

---

## Setting Up Your Tent

- **Pack light, pitch close.** Bring only what you need and set up near your neighbors. Think cozy village, not isolated fortress.
- **Smaller is sweeter.** Leave the four-room palace at home. Something humble is better for everyone's space and vibe.
- **We're building a kinky village.** You'll have space to decorate, co-create, and add your flavor to the communal corners. Come ready to play with the vibe.

---

## Electricity & Power

- **There is power.** We'll run electricity through the camp — bring your **longest extension cord** and a power strip.
- **But not too much.** No fridges, hair dryers, AC units, or anything with a heavy draw. You'll blow the grid. We're serious.
- Laptops and phone chargers: fine. Appliances: no.

---

## Weather & Practical Reality

- **It's August in Belgium.** Expect warm days and cooler nights. Bring layers for after dark.
- **It might rain.** Belgium. Pack a rain cover for your tent and gear. A tarp is genuinely useful.
- **No trenches.** If it rains, don't dig channels around your tent — we're guests on this land, and we leave it as we found it.
- **Sun protection matters.** SPF 30+ on exposed skin during the day. A hat helps.

---

## Facilities

- **Outdoor toilets** — clean and maintained
- **Showers** — shared, warm, available. Coordinate timing with kindness.
- **Firepit area** — for warmth, music, late-night conversation, and the rituals that don't happen before midnight

---

## Smoking & Fire

- Keep cigarettes (or whatever else you're smoking) away from tents
- Use the designated smoking/fire areas
- Bring a **portable ashtray** — no butts on the ground
- Respect fire safety at all times

---

## Can't Camp? Let's Talk.

We know camping isn't possible for everyone — accessibility, health, or just not-your-thing.

If you'd rather sleep off-site (nearby B&B, rental, or campervan parked outside the field), that's possible. We've done it before. What matters is that you're fully present during the day — not dropping in for highlights and disappearing.

If you're planning to sleep off-site, **let us know when you book** so we can talk it through and make good agreements together.

---

## Campervans & Vans

Bring yours, but let us know in advance. Space is limited and we want to make sure we can accommodate you. Email us before booking.
