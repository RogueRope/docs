## Before You Arrive

Take a breath. You're about to cross a threshold.

Maybe you're nervous. Maybe you're excited. Maybe both at once.  
That's exactly right. This is a big moment — you're choosing vulnerability, connection, presence.

**You belong here.**

This page will walk you through what happens when you arrive, where to go, who you'll meet, and how the first 24 hours unfold.

---

## Tuesday: Build Day (Optional, Strongly Encouraged)

**Arrival window:** Anytime from 10:00 onwards

If you can come on Tuesday, **please do.** Build Day is one of the most beautiful ways to arrive.

There's something intimate about shaping the space together before it holds us. Hanging fairy lights between trees. Setting up suspension points. Arranging the chill areas. Making coffee. Making eye contact. Making meaning.

You don't need skills. You need hands and a willingness to show up.

By the time camp officially opens Wednesday, the people who helped build it are already woven together.

---

## Wednesday Afternoon: Main Arrival

**Arrival window:** 14:00 onwards

This is the main arrival day. If Tuesday isn't possible, arrive Wednesday — but **be here by 21:00 for the Opening Circle without exception.**

### What happens when you arrive

1. **Find the welcome crew** — they'll be visible near the entrance
2. **Check in** — confirm your name, get your wristband and info packet
3. **Find your spot** — pick a place to pitch your tent
4. **Set up and settle** — give yourself time to land before diving into conversation
5. **Explore the space** — the play areas, the firepit, the chill spots
6. **Let yourself be welcomed** — people will find you

---

## Wednesday Evening: Opening Circle — 21:00

**This is mandatory.** If you cannot arrive in time for the Opening Circle, please let us know — and know that this may not be the right year for you to join.

The Opening Circle is how we arrive together — not just logistically, but energetically. It's where we set intention, hear agreements, and become a container together. It doesn't work if half the camp has already fragmented.

Please be there.

---

## The First 24 Hours

The first day can feel a lot. Here's what helps:

- **Go slow.** You don't need to meet everyone or do everything in the first few hours.
- **Eat something.** Dinner is served Wednesday evening. Be there.
- **Introduce yourself to one person.** Just one. Then another.
- **Let yourself be disoriented.** It's normal. It usually passes by morning.
- **Ask for what you need.** FWBs and organizers are visible and available.

By Thursday morning, most people have found their footing. Trust the process.

---

## If You're Arriving Nervous

That's okay.  
Most people arrive nervous.

The question isn't whether you'll fit in. You will.  
The question is which part of you will finally get to come out.

We've got you.
