# Editorial Quality Checklist

Framework for reviewing Hugo documentation content for clarity, tone consistency, narrative flow, readability, and editorial excellence.

## Quick Quality Assessment

For a rapid editorial review, check these essential elements:

- [ ] **Clarity**: Can someone unfamiliar with this topic understand it clearly?
- [ ] **Purpose**: Is the page's purpose clear from the first paragraph?
- [ ] **Audience**: Is it written for the intended audience level?
- [ ] **Flow**: Does information progress logically?
- [ ] **Tone**: Is the voice consistent with brand and audience expectations?
- [ ] **Completeness**: Are examples or illustrations needed?
- [ ] **Length**: Is it appropriately detailed for the topic?

## Comprehensive Editorial Quality Review

### 1. Clarity & Accessibility

**Headline & Opening**
- [ ] Title clearly states the topic or question being addressed
- [ ] Opening paragraph establishes context and purpose
- [ ] Reader knows immediately why they're reading this
- [ ] Technical terms are defined or assumed audience expertise is clear

**Content Structure**
- [ ] Information is organized logically (from general to specific, or simple to complex)
- [ ] Headings are descriptive and create a scannable outline
- [ ] Paragraphs are short and focused (ideally 3-4 sentences)
- [ ] No walls of text without visual breaks
- [ ] Lists are used effectively for multiple items

**Language**
- [ ] Sentences are concise and direct
- [ ] Jargon is explained or appropriate for audience
- [ ] Complex concepts are broken into smaller pieces
- [ ] Active voice is preferred over passive
- [ ] No unnecessary adjectives or marketing language

**Examples & Illustrations**
- [ ] Complex concepts include concrete examples
- [ ] Real-world applications are shown when helpful
- [ ] Visual breaks (lists, callouts, images) support understanding
- [ ] Examples match the audience's experience level

### 2. Tone & Voice Consistency

**Brand Voice Alignment**
- [ ] Tone matches your documentation's established voice
- [ ] Formality level is appropriate for topic and audience
- [ ] Word choices reflect brand personality
- [ ] Emotional resonance matches intent (supportive, informative, etc.)

**Perspective & Authority**
- [ ] Voice is consistent (not switching between first/second person randomly)
- [ ] Authority is appropriate (confident but not overconfident)
- [ ] No contradictory statements within the page or across sections
- [ ] Assumptions about reader knowledge are clear

**Engagement**
- [ ] Language invites reader engagement when appropriate
- [ ] Tone acknowledges reader concerns or questions
- [ ] Instructions are clear and approachable
- [ ] Warnings or cautions are appropriately emphasized

### 3. Narrative Flow & Coherence

**Information Progression**
- [ ] Content builds logically from foundational to advanced concepts
- [ ] Each section connects to the previous one
- [ ] Transitions between ideas are smooth
- [ ] No unexpected topic shifts
- [ ] Related concepts are grouped together

**Completeness & Sufficiency**
- [ ] All necessary information is included
- [ ] No major gaps in explanation
- [ ] Prerequisite knowledge is either provided or clearly assumed
- [ ] Sufficient context is given for understanding

**Coherence**
- [ ] All statements support the central purpose
- [ ] No contradictory information
- [ ] Cross-references to related content are accurate and helpful
- [ ] Conclusion or summary reinforces key points

### 4. Practical Usefulness

**Actionability**
- [ ] Instructions are step-by-step and clear
- [ ] Expected outcomes are described
- [ ] Edge cases or variations are addressed
- [ ] Troubleshooting guidance is provided if relevant

**Reference Value**
- [ ] Information can be easily skimmed for quick answers
- [ ] Headings serve as effective reference points
- [ ] Code samples or templates are copy-paste ready (if applicable)
- [ ] Important information stands out visually

**Audience Alignment**
- [ ] Content serves the stated audience purpose
- [ ] Examples and context match audience expertise
- [ ] Information depth is appropriate (not too basic, not too advanced)
- [ ] Pain points or common questions are addressed

### 5. Technical Accuracy & Currency

**Correctness**
- [ ] All facts are accurate
- [ ] Code examples are tested and functional
- [ ] Instructions produce the expected result
- [ ] Process descriptions match actual current practices

**Currency**
- [ ] Information reflects current best practices
- [ ] No references to deprecated or outdated approaches
- [ ] Dates or version numbers are current
- [ ] External references/links are still valid

**Completeness of Technical Details**
- [ ] Required context for code/instructions is provided
- [ ] Assumptions about setup or environment are stated
- [ ] Common errors or gotchas are mentioned
- [ ] Performance implications are noted when relevant

### 6. Formatting & Presentation

**Markdown & HTML**
- [ ] Proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3, no skipping levels)
- [ ] Consistent list formatting (bullets vs. numbered)
- [ ] Code blocks are properly formatted and syntax-highlighted
- [ ] Emphasis (bold, italic) is used appropriately, not excessively
- [ ] Special callouts (notes, warnings, tips) are used correctly

**Visual Hierarchy**
- [ ] Most important information is visually prominent
- [ ] Related content is visually grouped
- [ ] Too much emphasis decreases effectiveness (not everything can be important)
- [ ] Whitespace is used effectively

**Hugo-Specific**
- [ ] Shortcodes are used appropriately and correctly
- [ ] Frontmatter is complete and accurate
- [ ] Asset references (images, files) are correct
- [ ] No broken Hugo syntax or missing closing tags

## Editing Feedback Framework

When providing editorial feedback, follow this structure:

### 1. Overall Assessment
*One paragraph summary of the content's current state and primary editorial strengths/gaps*

Example: "This page clearly explains the concept and provides good examples. The structure is logical and scannable. Main opportunity: the practical application section could include a real-world scenario to help readers understand how this applies to them."

### 2. Specific Feedback by Category

**Clarity** (if issues found)
- Specific examples of unclear passages
- Suggested rewrites for clarity
- Questions that reveal gaps in explanation

**Tone** (if issues found)
- Examples of misaligned voice
- Specific suggestions for voice alignment
- Context on expected tone

**Flow** (if issues found)
- Where flow breaks down
- How information could be reordered
- Missing transitions or connections

**Completeness** (if issues found)
- What's missing
- Where examples would help
- What could be removed

### 3. Priority Recommendations

**Must-Do** (affects correctness or clarity)
- Factual errors to correct
- Major clarity issues
- Structural problems

**Should-Do** (improves quality significantly)
- Voice alignment issues
- Flow improvements
- Completeness gaps

**Nice-To-Do** (enhances polish)
- Minor wording improvements
- Formatting enhancements
- Style refinements

### 4. Specific Rewrite Suggestions

Provide concrete examples:
- Original: "The system can be configured in multiple ways."
- Suggested: "You can configure the system by editing the config.yaml file or using the web interface."

## Common Editorial Issues to Watch For

### Clarity Issues
- Passive voice hiding the subject
- Jargon without definition
- Sentences longer than 20 words
- Unclear pronouns (what does "it" refer to?)
- Assumptions about reader knowledge

### Tone Issues
- Inconsistent formality level
- Condescending or patronizing language
- Overly casual in formal documentation
- Corporate jargon conflicting with friendly voice
- Emotional language masking practical information

### Flow Issues
- Information in illogical order
- Missing transitions between paragraphs
- Abrupt topic changes
- Information presented before necessary context
- Conclusion doesn't follow from body

### Completeness Issues
- Steps are skipped or assumed
- Examples use domain-specific knowledge not explained
- Prerequisites are not stated
- Related information is scattered
- Edge cases or variations are not addressed

## Quick Review Process

For a rapid (15-minute) editorial review:

1. **Read for understanding** (5 min): Does the page make sense? Are there obvious clarity issues?
2. **Skim for structure** (3 min): Is information organized logically? Are headings effective?
3. **Check tone** (3 min): Does voice feel consistent and appropriate?
4. **Identify key gaps** (4 min): What's missing? What could be clearer?

Provide feedback in: Overall assessment + 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations.

## Deep Review Process

For comprehensive (45-60 minute) editorial review:

1. **Read carefully** (15 min): Understand content, identify clarity issues, note questions
2. **Check structure** (10 min): Evaluate organization, heading hierarchy, flow
3. **Assess tone** (10 min): Check voice consistency, audience alignment, engagement
4. **Verify completeness** (10 min): Are all necessary elements present? Are there gaps?
5. **Suggest improvements** (10 min): Prioritize feedback and write specific recommendations

Provide comprehensive feedback organized by editorial category with examples and rewrites.
